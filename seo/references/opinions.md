# Opinions and Hot Takes - Grout Academy

> The brand has a small number of strong, specific positions. Use them where relevant - they're the things that separate Grout Academy from the generic "we do regrouting in Geelong" competitor.
> Source: existing site copy, the /regrouting-geelong/ landing page, and the brand's "Simply Smarter" positioning.

## On products

- **Mapei or nothing.** Grout, silicone, waterproofing - all Mapei, every job, no exceptions. The reason Grout Academy uses Mapei is the same reason commercial bathrooms and pools specify it: it lasts longer, resists mould better, and bonds harder than the generic alternatives. The cost difference between premium and cheap product on a regrout is small. The cost difference in how long the job lasts is enormous.
- **Cheap product is the most expensive product over five years.** A budget regrout that fails in 18 months costs you the regrout twice. A Mapei regrout that lasts a decade costs you once.
- **"Premium" without naming the brand is a marketing word.** If a regrouting business won't tell you what they're putting in your shower, that's the answer.

## On the work itself

- **Regrouting is a strip-and-rebuild job, not a top-up.** Anyone offering to "freshen up" your grout by smearing new grout over the old is selling you the next failure. The mould lives inside the porous old grout. You have to take it out.
- **Cleaning grout is a stop-gap, not a fix.** If the lines are mouldy, the surface mould is what you can see; the mould you can't see is in the substrate. A bleach scrub buys you a fortnight. Regrouting fixes it.
- **If your tiles are loose, the grout failed first.** People assume loose tiles mean tile glue failure. Most of the time it's grout failure that let water behind the tiles, which then weakened the bond. Fix the grout, the tiles stay.
- **Silicone is not "grout that's flexible".** It goes in different places - the corners, floor-wall joints, anywhere two surfaces meet at right angles. Silicone has to be replaced when grout is replaced, otherwise the new grout cracks within a year as the building moves.

## On the industry

- **Most "regrouting" quotes are actually re-silicone quotes.** A re-silicone job is a couple of hours and a few hundred dollars. A regrout is a couple of days and is properly priced. If a quote sounds suspiciously cheap for a regrout, ask exactly what's being removed.
- **The cheapest quote is the most expensive outcome.** This is a tradesman cliche because it's true. A regrout done with cheap product, by someone who doesn't strip the old grout out properly, fails inside two years. You then pay a second tradie to do the job properly.
- **"Free quote" should be exactly that.** Some operators use the free quote as a foot-in-the-door for a hard sell. The brand's quote process: come look, fixed price written down, decide in your own time. No follow-up calls, no "limited-time pricing".

## On who should NOT hire Grout Academy

- **If your grout is fine and you just want it cleaner, hire a tile and grout cleaner, not us.** Cleaning is not regrouting. We don't pretend a regrout is what you need if it isn't.
- **If you want the absolute lowest price, we are not it.** The brand prices on Mapei product cost and the time it takes to do the job properly. There are operators in Geelong who quote less. They're using cheaper product, taking less time, or both. Either get a quote from one of them and do it twice, or get a quote from us and do it once.
- **If your tiles are cracked across most of the bathroom, you might need a re-tile, not a regrout.** A regrout fixes grout. If 60% of your tiles are damaged, the cheaper long-term move is a fresh tile job. We'll tell you if that's where you've landed.
- **If you want same-week service in peak season, we usually can't.** The standard booking lead time is one to two weeks. Quality regrouting takes time and the calendar is what it is.

## On pricing

- **Fixed quote in writing before work starts.** Not "from $X". Not "estimated". A number the customer can hold us to.
- **No call-out fee.** The quote visit is free.
- **Pricing depends on three things, in this order:** size of the area, condition of the existing grout, whether silicone and waterproofing top-ups are needed. Anyone quoting before seeing the job is guessing.

## On older homes

- **Pre-1990 Geelong bathrooms are a different job from a 2010 build.** Older floor wastes, older substrates, older waterproofing. Sometimes there isn't waterproofing under the tiles at all - just tiles on cement render. We tell you what we find before we start, and we adjust the quote if the underlying picture is worse than the surface suggested.
- **A bathroom that's been regrouted before by someone else is harder than a virgin job.** The old work has to come out cleanly without damaging the tiles. Budget more time, not less.

## How to use opinions in content

- **One strong opinion per blog post or service page, max.** Drop it where the post would otherwise feel generic. Two opinions in one post starts to read like a manifesto.
- **Always back the opinion with a number, a product name, or a concrete consequence.** "Cheap product fails fast" is a bumper sticker. "A regrout done with budget grout fails in 18 months. A Mapei regrout lasts ten years. Same labour cost, ten times the lifespan" is an opinion.
- **Use the "when not to hire us" section liberally.** Telling readers we're not the right fit for cheap-and-fast jobs is the single biggest voice tell that separates Grout Academy from generic AI content. Generic content will never tell a reader to walk away.
