# Voice - Grout Academy

> Read this file before writing any page of content for Grout Academy.
> Then read [`humour.md`](./humour.md), [`stats.md`](./stats.md), [`opinions.md`](./opinions.md), and [`stories.md`](./stories.md). All five must be read before any content goes live.

## Who is writing

The owner of Grout Academy stays anonymous. There is no personal byline, no "I" voice, no name, no photo of a person. Content is always written as the business: "we", "our team", "Grout Academy".

This is deliberate. The brand sells competence and reliability, not personality. The reader is choosing a tradie they can trust to fix their shower properly, not a personality they want to follow.

The voice is older Aussie tradesman. Twenty-plus years of fixing what other people botched. Plain-spoken. Doesn't oversell. Doesn't undersell. Tells you what's wrong, what we'll do about it, and how long it takes. Then gets on with the job.

Not Marco-the-plumber-with-dad-jokes. Closer to a quiet, capable older worker who turns up on time, leaves the bathroom cleaner than he found it, and answers questions straight.

## Sentence rhythm

- **Short sentences.** Mostly under 18 words. Long ones are rare and load-bearing.
- **One idea per sentence.** If you need "and" twice, split it.
- **Start with the answer.** Then add context. Never the other way around.
- **Declarative, not promotional.** State the fact, move on. Don't dress it up.

Example of rhythm pulled from the existing site:

> Damaged grout in showers leads to leaks, mould growth and water damage to surrounding walls and floors. Professional shower regrouting restores both the appearance and waterproof integrity of your shower, protecting your home from costly damage.

Notice: problem first. Mechanism. Outcome. No adjectives doing emotional work. No hedging.

Another rhythm pattern from the site:

> Water gets in, grout cracks, and the seal breaks down. Poor installation, settling, and age all play a role. We strip it out and do it right.

Three short sentences. Each one earns its place. The last one is the only place "we" appears - the action belongs to the business.

## Words and phrases the brand uses

- **"we" / "our team"** - never "I", never the owner's name
- **"premium Mapei products"** / **"Mapei grout"** / **"Mapei systems"** - product brand is named explicitly
- **"professional"** - used sparingly, only where the contrast with DIY or cowboy work is meaningful
- **"strip it back"** / **"strip it out"** - regrouting is a remove-and-rebuild job, not a top-up
- **"do it right"** / **"do it properly"** / **"do the job properly"** - the brand promise
- **"fixed quote"** / **"free quote"** - pricing is named, no "starting at"
- **"call us"** / **"give us a call"** - never "contact us" or "reach out"
- **"the work speaks for itself"** / **"the work speaks louder than words"** - explicit on the homepage, lean on this
- **"honest workmanship"** - claimed on the LP, defines the brand
- **"long-lasting"** / **"long-term results"** - the value pitch is durability over price
- **"we come back and sort it"** - the warranty position
- **"word-of-mouth"** - how the business grows; mention where natural
- **"across Geelong, Melbourne, Ballarat and surrounding regions"** - the standard service-area phrasing
- **"one to two days"** for shower regrout duration - canonical timeframe
- **"within 24 hours"** for quote turnaround
- **"one to two weeks"** for booking lead time

## Words and phrases the brand never uses

- **Em dashes (-)** - hyphens, commas, or full stops only
- **Exclamation marks** - never. The voice is calm, not excited.
- **Emojis** - never. In any client-facing copy.
- **"Unlock", "leverage", "empower", "synergy", "seamless", "cutting-edge", "world-class", "best-in-class"** - empty signals
- **"In today's fast-paced world"** or any variant
- **"We pride ourselves on"** - the brand shows, doesn't claim
- **"Comprehensive solution"** / **"end-to-end"** / **"holistic"** - corporate filler
- **"Affordable"**, **"competitive pricing"**, **"value for money"** - empty signals; if pricing is the point, give a number or describe the model (fixed quote, no surprises)
- **"Reach out"** - say "call" or "email"
- **"Utilise"** - "use"
- **"Robust"** / **"premium quality"** as a stand-alone claim - either name the product (Mapei) or describe what makes it durable
- **"Industry-leading"** - empty
- **First-person ("I")** - the owner is anonymous, the brand voice is plural
- **"Cheap"** as a positive - if budget is the point, say "fixed price" or "no surprises"
- **Slang or trendy phrasing** - "obsessed with", "absolute game changer", "legend" - not the brand register

## Formatting rules

- **Headings are statements, not labels.** "Why Shower Regrouting Matters" works. "Our Process" is dead. Headings can deliver an answer or set up a fact.
- **Use "we" for the business throughout.** Never switch to "I". Never name the owner.
- **Real numbers over adjectives.** "One to two days", not "fast turnaround". "Premium Mapei products" (named brand), not "premium quality materials" (vague).
- **Bullets for lists of three or more items.** Tight bullets, no full sentences trailing.
- **Bold key phrases sparingly.** A heading is already strong; bold inside paragraphs is for the one phrase a skim reader needs.
- **No bracketed asides or stage directions.** The voice doesn't wink at the reader.
- **Short paragraphs.** One to four sentences. White space carries weight on a service site.

## Page structure (service / blog page default)

1. **H1 with primary keyword + city** (e.g. "Shower Regrouting Geelong")
2. **One-paragraph hero subhead.** Problem named, solution stated, service area named.
3. **Why it matters** - what happens if you don't act
4. **Our process** - 4 to 6 short bullets, action verbs first
5. **Signs you need this service** - 4 to 6 short bullets, observable symptoms
6. **Why choose Grout Academy** - 3 to 4 short bullets, no marketing fluff
7. **Cross-sell to related services** (3-card grid)
8. **CTA strip** - "Get a Free Quote" / "Call 0418 958 131"
9. **FAQs** - 5 to 8 questions, direct 2 to 4 sentence answers (also wired to FAQPage schema)
10. **Footer**

## Tells that it's AI-written (delete and rewrite if you see these)

- "Whether you're [X], [Y], or [Z]..." intro sentences
- "Looking for [service] in [city]? Look no further."
- Three-item lists where every item starts with an "-ing" verb ("Ensuring quality, delivering excellence, providing...")
- Paragraphs that end with a rhetorical flourish instead of a fact
- Parallel sentence structures repeating across paragraphs
- The word "comprehensive" anywhere
- Bullet points where every bullet is a full sentence with a period
- A closing sentence that restates the opening
- Generic adjective stacks ("professional, reliable, trustworthy service")
- Any reference to "passion", "passionate about", or "love what we do"

If you catch any of the above, delete the paragraph and start again.

## One-line summary

**Write like a trusted older tradie answering a homeowner's question at the front door. Plain. Direct. No hype. Tells you what's wrong, what we'll do, and what it takes.**
