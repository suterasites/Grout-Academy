New Estates, Cheap Grout, Early Failure

Bannockburn has grown fast. A lot of the bathrooms we look at are five to ten years old, in tidy estate homes that still feel new from the outside. The tiles are fine. The fixtures are fine. The grout and silicone are not.

Volume builders use the cheapest grout and silicone that meets spec. It passes handover. It looks fine for a few years. Then it starts cracking at the corners, the silicone goes black at the floor-wall joint, and mould moves into the grout lines you cannot scrub clean.

The other half of our work out here is the older rural homes - farmhouses and bush properties that are decades older than the estate. Different problem, same fix. Strip the old grout and silicone out. Rebuild with Mapei. Do it once.

FAQs - Bannockburn

Common Questions

Is Bannockburn a long trip for you?

No. Bannockburn sits between Geelong and Ballarat, both regular service areas. Travel is built into the quote, no separate call-out fee.

Why is my five-year-old Bannockburn estate bathroom already failing?

Volume builders use the cheapest grout and silicone that meets spec. They look fine on handover and start cracking and growing mould inside five to seven years, even when the rest of the build is solid. The cost difference between cheap grout and Mapei on a regrout is small. The cost difference in how long it lasts is enormous.

Do you cover Inverleigh, Lethbridge, Teesdale?

Yes. Bannockburn, Inverleigh, Lethbridge, Teesdale, Meredith, Shelford, Rokewood and the surrounding Golden Plains and Moorabool areas are all standard service area.

Can you do an older rural farmhouse bathroom?

Yes. Older rural bathrooms often have substrates and waterproofing that pre-date current standards. We tell you what we find before we start and adjust the quote in writing if the underlying picture is worse than the surface suggested.

How quickly can you start a Bannockburn job?

Quotes within 24 hours. Standard booking lead time is one to two weeks. If we are already in the area for another job that week, sometimes sooner.
